About Me!

I started woodworking when I was about 6, following my dad around the garage to "help". As I got older, I had ideas of my own that we worked on together until I knew my way around the shop enough to make a few things myself. One summer I wanted to experiment with combining wood burning and stains on a box we were making together. It didn't exactly go smoothly, but it worked, so I used that method to make gifts for my friends and family, slowly perfecting the process and learning with each project. Eventually my Dad got tired of sizing and framing my "canvases" all the way from Michigan, so he got me a few of my own tools. I had graduated, was waiting for my job to hire me back, and bored out of my mind, so I jumped in head first with new art designs. I started at farmer's markets and then Christmas shows, eventually shifting to laser-engraving to keep up with demand.

I had graduated, was waiting for my job to hire me back, and bored out of my mind, so I jumped in head first with new art designs. I started at farmer's markets and then Christmas shows, eventually shifting to laser-engraving to keep up with demand. However, I got more and more interested in exotic woods and the wonderful colors that nature is capable of. I started making pendants, earrings and cutting boards to work with more interesting species than pine and was amazed and the beautiful natural colors. Although now that I'm in year 3, I'd have to say that I love working with exotic woods as much as I love making new art pieces.
